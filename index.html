Strength • STR5DULS

Training Focus Overview

▼

Strength

Focuses on Motor Unit Recruitment - teaching your body to produce as much force as possible with the muscles you already have, not necessarily about making them bigger.

Progressive overload is very important for strength training - you have to give your body a slightly bigger challenge over time (this is progressive overload).

  • 85–95% of your 1-Rep Max (1RM) for 3-5 sets — 1-5 reps — 3–5 minute rest
  • Great modifiers for lack of equipment include: Unilateral (single-limb) variations — creating deeper/fuller/longer range of motion (ROM), like deficit variations

Hypertrophy (Muscle Size)

Focuses on building muscle size and "armor" so you look more muscular and have more tissue protecting and supporting your joints.

  • 65–80% of your 1-Rep Max (1RM) for 3–5 sets — 6–15 reps — 60–90 second rest (a bit of burn and pump is the goal here)

Primary levers for this goal (especially with limited equipment) are tempo and position modifiers in the hardest, most stretched part of the rep:

  • Iso-holds (Yielding Isometrics) – hold still and squeeze in that "bottom" or mid-range position for 10–30 seconds per set
  • Pauses – briefly stop (1–3 seconds) at the bottom or midpoint of each rep to kill momentum
  • Pulses – short mini-reps in a small range around the bottom position (no full lockout) to keep constant tension
  • 1.5 reps – do a full rep, come halfway back, then drop into the bottom again and stand all the way up

Power & Velocity (The "Pop")

Focuses on teaching your body to use the strength you already have quickly—how fast you can turn force on, not how much weight you can grind.

  • Roughly 30–60% of your 1-Rep Max (1RM) for 3–6 sets — 1–5 explosive reps — 2–3 minute rest (every rep should be fast; stop the set if speed drops)

Modifiers include:

  • Concentric-only emphasis – control the lower phase of the rep (eccentric), but make the "up" phase (concentric) as fast as possible
  • Speed-isos (Overcoming/Pushing Isometrics) – 1–4 second pushing against an immoveable object / can be paired with fast reps after the push

Conditioning (Work Capacity & Engine Management)

  • Aerobic Base (Zone 2): easy, steady cardio that builds your "all-day engine" and recovery. 60–75% HRmax or a pace where you can talk in full sentences — 20–60 minutes steady
  • Tempo / Threshold: comfortably hard cardio that raises your sustained pace before you start gassing. Roughly 80–90% HRmax (talking becomes difficult) — Continuous 10–30 minutes, or intervals like 3–10 min work / 1–2 min easy
  • VO₂ Max: hard intervals that expand your top-end cardio ceiling. 2–5 minutes hard / 2–5 minutes easy, repeated — Total hard time 12–25 minutes
  • Anaerobic / Sprint: short, very hard bursts that train explosive repeat effort and "redline tolerance." 10–60 seconds very hard, with rest 2–5× work duration
